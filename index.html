Active Riders

How many riders include bike sharing as a regular part of their commute?

Regular riders are defined as riders that have Flex passes. Using number of trips as the count, there are 7200 riders (upper bound) who use bike share regularly for their commute. This serves as an upper bound because this is the number of trips that had Flex passes, which will be greater than the number of users. We will have 7200 users with a Flex pass only if each trip was with a unique user.

Trends by Season

Quarters will be used to explore seasonal changes. All data is from quarters 3 and 4.

i. Looking at total rides per quarter:

One-way trips are more popular than round trips across all passholder types. This could possibly be due to the flexibility that one-way trips provide- for example, a rider could bike to their first class, then take another bike to their second class.

Trip Route-Passholder Combos

Trip Routes: One Way, Round Trip

Passholder Types: Pass (365 days), Monthly Pass, Walk-up

One-way trips are more popular than round trips across all passholder types. This could possibly be due to the flexibility that one-way trips provide- for example, a rider could bike to their first class, then take another bike to their second class.

The most popular combination is the One-way monthly pass combination. Bike sharing is very popular among milennials, and provides a cheap form of transportation. Milennials who use bikeshare as a part of their regular commute to school may be more incentivized to buy a monthly pass because school is not year round. They may also use it to travel from place to place on campus, meaning more flexibility is needed, thus giving a popular push to one-way trips.
